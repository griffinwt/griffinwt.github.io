500 Cities: Local Data for Better Health

Recently I've been practicing my Tableau visualization skills using a publicly available dataset provided by the CDC "related to unhealthy behaviors (5), health outcomes (13), and use of preventive services (9)." In particular, I've been looking at one of the "unhealthy behavior" metrics which is respondents reporting that they sleep less than 7 hours per night. Lack of sleep has been associated with "numberous chronic diseases and conditions" as well as accidents, industrial errors, and low quality of life, yet I, like many others, often find myself undervaluing this critical rest period. After grouping the neighborhood-sized samples into cities, I'm building a map to examine how responses vary across the USA.

Disaster Tweets - Binary Classification Using NLP

While I'm not personally active on Twitter, I was intrigued by this Kaggle competition entitled "Natural Language Processing with Disaster Tweets". In essence, the goal is binary classification of messages using Natural Language Processing to distinguish between tweets about actual natural disasters compared to those using similar keywords metaphorically. It has been a great opportunity to run a variety of models in search of the most accurate solution.
